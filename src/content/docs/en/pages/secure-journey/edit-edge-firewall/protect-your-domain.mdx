Once you create a firewall with Azion, you can modify a few configurations.

In this guide, you'll learn how to select a firewall to protect a particular domain.

On the Console, you must now configure your domain so your firewall protects it.

The steps depend on how your account is set up: accounts using the new [Workloads](/en/documentation/build/workloads/) product configure the firewall in the workload's settings, while accounts still using the legacy [Domains](/en/documentation/build/applications/reference/domains/) settings configure it in the domain's settings. Choose the tab that matches your account.

:::tip
If you're not sure which steps apply to your account, see [the Verify Your Account Migration guide](/en/documentation/account/guides/verify-account-migration/) to determine if your account has already been migrated.
:::

<Tabs client:visible>
    <Fragment slot="tab.consoleworkloads">Console - Workloads</Fragment>
    <Fragment slot="tab.consoledomains">Console - Domains</Fragment>


<Fragment slot="panel.consoleworkloads">

1. On the **Products menu**, select **Workloads**.
2. Click on the Workload you want to protect with your firewall.
3. In the **Deployment Settings** section, click the `Firewall` selector and choose the firewall.
4. Click the **Save** button.

</Fragment>

<Fragment slot="panel.consoledomains">

1. On the **Products menu**, select **Domains**.
2. Click on the domain you want to protect with your firewall.
3. In the **Settings** section, click the `Firewall` selector and choose the firewall.
4. Click the **Save** button.

Done. Now the selected firewall will protect the domain you indicated.

</Fragment>
